Smart move—sticking to the original "Sacred Valley First" plan is the best way to ensure the whole family (especially the kids) stays energized and altitude-sickness free. By dropping down to the Valley immediately after landing in Cusco, you’re essentially giving everyone a "soft landing" before tackling the higher elevation of Cusco later.

Here is the finalized itinerary, polished for a family of six:

The "Altitude-Smart" 2-Week Peru Itinerary
Phase 1: Lima (3 Days)
Day 1: Coastal Welcome. Land in Lima, check into Miraflores. Walk the Malecón for Pacific views.

Day 2: Culture & Catacombs. Visit the Plaza de Armas and the San Francisco Monastery catacombs (usually a hit with the 13 & 17-year-olds).

Day 3: Barranco & Food. Explore the street art in Barranco. Grab a world-class lunch (try Causa or Lomo Saltado). Fly to Cusco in the late afternoon, but take a pre-arranged van straight down to the Sacred Valley (1.5-hour drive).

Phase 2: The Sacred Valley (3 Days)
Day 4: Pisac Ruins. Explore the massive Incan terraces and the famous market. Stay in a "hacienda-style" hotel to relax.

Day 5: Maras & Moray. See the Maras Salt Mines (over 3,000 salt pans) and the circular ruins of Moray. Consider an ATV tour here to keep the 19 & 22-year-olds engaged.

Day 6: Ollantaytambo. Explore this "living Incan town." It’s the best-preserved example of Incan town planning.

Phase 3: Machu Picchu (2 Days)
Day 7: The Train Journey. Take the Vistadome train from Ollantaytambo to Aguas Calientes. Explore the town and prep for the big day.

Day 8: The Lost City. Head up to Machu Picchu early. If the kids are fit, book the Huayna Picchu hike (limited tickets, book months ahead!). Take the evening train back toward Cusco.

Phase 4: Cusco (5 Days)
Day 9: Settling into the Heights. Since you’ve spent 5 days at lower altitudes, the 3,400m of Cusco will be much easier. Relax at the Plaza de Armas.

Day 10: Saqsaywaman. Visit the fortress of "Sexy Woman" (as locals joke). The stones are astronomical in size—great for family photos.

Day 11: San Blas & Artisans. Wander the blue-doored streets of San Blas. Visit the Chocolate Museum for a workshop.

Day 12: Adventure/Rainbow Mountain. This is a grueling 4:00 AM start for a high-altitude hike. It’s optional—some of the group might prefer a spa day or more museum time.

Day 13: Final Shopping. Hit the San Pedro Market for last-minute alpaca sweaters and souvenirs. Farewell dinner with live Andean music.

Day 14: Departure. Morning flight Cusco → Lima → Home.
